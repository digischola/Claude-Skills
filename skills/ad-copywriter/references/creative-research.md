# Creative Research — Performance Intelligence for Ad Creative

Load this file in Step 6 (Image Prompts) and Step 7 (Video Storyboards) of the ad-copywriter skill. Contains research-backed rules that override default aesthetic assumptions.

Source: Perplexity deep research (2026-04-16). Meta Marketing Science, VidMob (5M+ video ads), CreativeX, Nielsen, peer-reviewed studies. Confidence labels per finding.

---

## 1. Scroll-Stop Rules (Image + Video Frame 1)

### 1.1 Attention Window
- Users spend ~1.7 seconds with mobile feed content. Can recall after 0.25s exposure. [HIGH]
- 47% of video campaign value delivered in first 3 seconds. 74% in first 10 seconds. [HIGH]
- Hook, brand cue, and primary benefit must all be visible in the first frame/scene.

### 1.2 Contrast & Brightness
- High contrast creatives: +32% CTR, -21% CPC vs muted schemes. [HIGH]
- Light/mid-light backgrounds significantly outperform dark: +70% purchase rate for light vs dark. [MEDIUM]
- **Rule: Default to light backgrounds with dark text/subject. Allow darker moods for architecture/luxury but keep focal element clearly separated.**

### 1.3 Color Temperature by Vertical
- Warm tones (amber, wood, skin): +40% better in personal care/wellness. [MEDIUM]
- Cool tones (blue, green, steel): +60% better in beauty, also strong for B2B/architecture. [MEDIUM]
- **Architecture/design:** cooler desaturated palettes (stone, glass, steel) + warm accents (wood, lighting) to avoid sterility.
- **Wellness/retreats:** greens, teals, warm natural tones (sunrise, wood). No harsh neons.
- **Local services / B2B:** neutral backgrounds (white, light grey, soft blue) + warm accent on CTAs.

### 1.4 Faces
- Human faces: robust positive effect on engagement. Increases with larger on-screen face size. [HIGH]
- Architecture/design/high-ticket: faces useful in UGC/testimonials but pure product imagery often outperforms for direct response. Use sparingly. [MEDIUM]
- **Wellness/retreats, local services, B2B:** faces build trust. Owner/expert faces especially strong.

### 1.5 Gaze Direction
- Averted gaze (looking into scene): better for emotional/lifestyle ads — enhances narrative transportation, improves purchase intent. [HIGH — peer-reviewed]
- Direct gaze (to camera): better for informative/expert/B2B ads — enhances credibility. [HIGH]
- Subject looking AT the product: increases product attention and brand memorization. [HIGH]
- **Rule: Wellness/retreats → averted gaze. B2B/expert → direct gaze. Product showcase → gaze toward product.**

### 1.6 Visual Complexity
- Moderate color complexity increases engagement. [MEDIUM]
- Extremely complex visuals reduce clarity. Inverted-U relationship.
- **Rule: One dominant focal element + 1-2 supporting elements. Pattern interrupt via unexpected framing (cropped close-ups, off-center subject, unusual angles) WITHOUT cluttering the frame.**

### 1.7 Text on Images
- Meta: less text generally performs better. Old 20% rule softened but still directional. [HIGH]
- VidMob: clear text in first 3 seconds of video → +46% purchase rate. [HIGH]
- Google Display: explicitly says "avoid overlaid text" — unreadable at small sizes, redundant with headlines. [HIGH]
- **Rule: Meta images — allow concise, high-contrast text overlay (headline or offer) in modest area. Google Display — NO text overlay. Video — lead with text in first frame.**

### 1.8 Brightness & Negative Space
- Light images: +70% purchase rate vs dark. Neutral +39% vs dark. [MEDIUM]
- Google: recommends against excessive blank space (>80% of frame). Product should be the main focus. [HIGH]
- **Rule: Subject occupies 50-70% of frame. Leave 20-30% for text zone (Meta) or breathing room (Google). Never >80% empty.**

## 2. Meta Creative Performance Patterns

### 2.1 Format Performance
- Video: 35-50% higher CTR than static but higher CPMs. 48% higher sales rates in retail. [MEDIUM]
- Static: 40-60% lower CPM. Often lower CPA in retargeting. [MEDIUM]
- Video wins cold prospecting lead gen (higher engagement, stronger qualification).
- Static/carousel wins retargeting (clear offer, trust established).
- **Rule: Prospecting → video or single image (test both). Retargeting → static image or carousel.**

### 2.2 Aspect Ratios
- 4:5 outperforms 1:1 in mobile feed by 10-30% CTR (more screen real estate). [MEDIUM]
- 9:16 required for Stories/Reels. Lower CPM but more volatile CPA. [MEDIUM]
- **Rule: Feed default → 4:5. Stories/Reels → 9:16. Square 1:1 as fallback only.**

### 2.3 Video Length
- 10-15 seconds: +19% purchase rate vs average. 184% better than 1-5s. 12% better than 16s+. [MEDIUM]
- Stories/Reels lead gen: 10-15 seconds with hook in first 1-2s and CTA repeated.
- Feed complex offer: 15-30 seconds OK if strong first 3 seconds.

### 2.4 UGC vs Polished
- UGC-style: +32% efficiency, +9% incremental reach vs non-UGC. [MEDIUM]
- Authenticity is the driver — overly polished "fake UGC" underperforms.
- **Architecture/luxury exception: editorial photography is the expected register. UGC works for testimonials/walk-throughs but not for hero product shots.**

### 2.5 Creative Fatigue
- After ~4 exposures: conversion drops ~45%, CTR drops ~23%. [MEDIUM]
- By frequency 9: CTR drops ~50%. CPA increases 60%+.
- Refresh static every 20-30 days. Video every 40-60 days.
- **Rule: Plan 3-6 distinct creative concepts per campaign for rotation.**

### 2.6 Sound
- 85% of Facebook video views happen sound-off. [HIGH]
- Captions: +12% view time, +30% completion rate. [HIGH]
- Design every video for complete comprehension without audio. Captions mandatory.

### 2.7 Carousel
- Optimal: 3-5 cards. First card = strongest hook (not a "mystery" card). [LOW-MEDIUM]
- Outperforms single images for catalog-style/multi-feature products.
- Can underperform video in TOF campaigns.

## 3. Google Display & Demand Gen Patterns

### 3.1 Image + Video Together
- Advertisers running both: +6% more conversions per dollar vs image-only. [HIGH — Google data]
- **Rule: Always provide both image and video assets in Demand Gen campaigns.**

### 3.2 Text Overlay
- Google explicitly: "Avoid overlaid text. Avoid overlaid logos. Avoid overlaid buttons." [HIGH]
- Text may be unreadable at smaller sizes and redundant with headlines.
- **Rule: Google Display/Demand Gen image prompts should NEVER include text overlay zone instructions. Clean product/outcome imagery only.**

### 3.3 Asset Volume
- Upload all ratios: 1.91:1 landscape + 1:1 square + 4:5 portrait. [HIGH]
- Up to 15 marketing images recommended for responsive display. [HIGH]
- Rule of three: at least 3 images per aspect ratio per ad group.

## 4. Industry-Specific Visual Patterns

### 4.1 Architecture / Design / Luxury
- Editorial photography with natural light, clean lines, minimal clutter → expected register. [MEDIUM]
- Interior shots (kitchens, living spaces, bathrooms) → higher engagement than pure exteriors. [MEDIUM]
- Wide establishing shots → better primary scroll-stop assets. Detail shots → better for carousels and retargeting. [MEDIUM]
- Showing explicit high prices in TOF creative → can deter clicks for luxury. Price discussed later. [MEDIUM]
- Award badges / media logos → improve CTR when relevant audience. [LOW-MEDIUM]
- 3D renders: accepted but real/realistic photos preferred for trust in high-ticket. [MEDIUM]

### 4.2 Wellness / Retreats / Hospitality
- People experiencing the environment → converts better for bookings than pure landscapes. [MEDIUM]
- Calm, minimal compositions with greens/blues → aligns with relaxation goals. [MEDIUM]
- Seasonally aligned creative → improves response for time-bound offers. [MEDIUM]
- Testimonial overlays: place testimonial in primary text, not heavy text on image. [MEDIUM]

### 4.3 Local Services
- Before/after transformation imagery → strong scroll-stop AND proof. [LOW-MEDIUM]
- Owner/team faces on-site → builds local trust. [LOW-MEDIUM]
- Recognizable local landmarks → increases relevance and CTR. [LOW-MEDIUM]

### 4.4 B2B / Professional Services
- Realistic imagery of workflows/dashboards/people outperforms abstract illustration for conversion. [MEDIUM]
- Expert faces (founders, consultants) with direct gaze → powerful for lead gen. [MEDIUM]
- Data visualization: simple charts can scroll-stop for analytical audiences but keep complexity low. [MEDIUM]

## 5. AI Image Generation for Ads

### 5.1 Performance
- AI-generated images can perform as well as or better than real photos in paid ads. [MEDIUM]
- Acceptance drops when viewers learn the image is AI-generated. Realism is key.
- **Rule: Aim for photorealism. Never disclose AI origin unless required by policy (political/social issue ads on Meta).**

### 5.2 Prompt Structure for Photorealism
- Include: "ultra-realistic photograph," "cinematic lighting," "shot on [lens]mm"
- Specify precise lens (35mm for establishing, 85mm for portrait) and aperture (shallow DOF)
- Real location types, not vague "beautiful background"
- **Rule: Keep prompts to 1-3 concise descriptive sentences + aspect ratio + composition instruction. Overly long prompts produce noisy/inconsistent results.**

### 5.3 Negative Space for Text Overlay
- AI models partially respect "leave space for text" instructions. Adherence is imperfect.
- **Practical fix: Generate two versions — centered subject (for organic/Google) and off-center subject (for Meta with text overlay).**

### 5.4 Reference Image Strategy
- Use image-to-image or "generate more like this" features to maintain product consistency.
- For specific products AI has never seen: combine AI backgrounds with real product shots in post-production.
- **Rule: Note in image prompts that the client should composite real product photos onto AI-generated environments when needed.**

### 5.5 Common Artifacts to Avoid
- Uncanny faces, extra fingers, warped architecture, incorrect text on signage.
- **Rule: For architecture prompts, specify "accurate architectural perspective, no warped lines" explicitly. Avoid requesting text ON the generated image — add it in post.**

### 5.6 Style Consistency
- Same base prompt template (camera, lighting, background descriptors) across all images in a campaign.
- Vary only: subject, camera angle, and minor scene details.
- Consistent color palette from brand guidelines.

## 6. Video Creative Science

### 6.1 Hook Patterns (First 3 Seconds)
- Immediate outcome reveal (before/after jump-cut): strong for services.
- Bold value prop or question in large text: strong for lead gen.
- Face to camera with direct address: strong for B2B/informative.
- Experiential POV shots: strong for wellness/lifestyle.
- Good hook rate benchmark: 25-35%. Top performers: >30%. [MEDIUM]

### 6.2 Pacing
- 15s ads: scene changes every 1-3 seconds.
- 30s ads: 2-4 second scenes, slower OK if narrative is strong.
- Fast-paced for Reels/TikTok. Slower acceptable for complex B2B.

### 6.3 Text in Video
- Text in first 3 seconds: +46% purchase rate. [HIGH]
- Large, high-contrast fonts. Combine audio + on-screen text + captions.

### 6.4 CTA Placement
- Introduce CTA early AND repeat at end. Don't rely on end-card only.
- "Shop" wording outperforms "get/find" for purchase. [MEDIUM]

### 6.5 Thumbnail = Standalone Creative
- Treat first frame as if it were a static ad: high contrast, clear subject, minimal text, visible face or transformation.

## 7. Creative Testing Rules

### 7.1 Testing Hierarchy (Test First → Test Last)
1. Concept/angle (core promise, narrative)
2. Format (static vs video vs carousel; UGC vs polished)
3. Hook / opening frame (first 3 seconds)
4. Visual style and color palette
5. Copy and CTA language

### 7.2 Early Winner Signals
- Hook rate ≥25-30% → promote to scaling
- Above-median CTR → promote
- Healthy hold rates (25%/50% video completion) → promote
- Don't wait for full CPA data. Use leading indicators.

### 7.3 Volume
- Small budgets: 3-6 distinct concepts per ad set.
- Scaled: 15-30 new creatives per month. Batch 12-20 per testing cycle.
- Avoid small cosmetic tweaks — Meta penalizes creative similarity.
