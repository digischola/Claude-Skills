# Sector Lens: Local Services

Additional data points to extract when analyzing local service businesses — restaurants, cafes, cinemas, temples, community centers, salons, gyms, retail stores.

Load this lens during Step 2 when the business operates primarily from a physical location serving a local catchment area.

---

## Extra Extraction Points (Beyond Universal Framework)

### Location & Access
- Number of locations
- Address and neighborhood context
- Parking availability
- Public transport accessibility
- Delivery/service radius (if applicable)
- Operating hours (weekday vs weekend)

### Menu / Service List
- Full menu or service list with pricing
- Popular items / signature offerings
- Dietary accommodations (restaurants: vegan, gluten-free, halal, kosher)
- Service tiers or membership levels (gyms, salons)
- Special packages (events, group bookings)

### Local Presence Signals
- Google Business Profile: rating, review count, response rate, photos
- Google Maps visibility and category accuracy
- Local directory listings (Yelp, TripAdvisor, Zomato, etc.)
- Community involvement (sponsorships, events, partnerships)
- Local press or media mentions

### Foot Traffic & Discovery
- How do customers find them (walk-in, search, social, referral)
- Signage and street visibility
- Proximity to complementary businesses
- Event calendar or community programming
- Seasonal foot traffic patterns

### Customer Experience Signals
- Review themes (what customers praise or complain about)
- Response to negative reviews (tone, speed, resolution)
- Loyalty programs or repeat customer incentives
- Reservation/booking system
- Wait times or availability constraints

### Community & Cultural Context (Temples, Cultural Orgs)
- Community size and demographics
- Event calendar (festivals, celebrations, weekly services)
- Donation model and fundraising approach
- Volunteer base and engagement
- Cultural education programs
- Diaspora community connections

### Competitive Local Landscape
- Direct competitors within catchment radius
- Indirect competitors (different format, same need)
- Price positioning vs local alternatives
- Online ordering/delivery platform presence (restaurants)
- Aggregator visibility (ClassPass for gyms, OpenTable for restaurants)

---

## Common BLANK Fields for Local Services

These frequently need client intake or direct observation:
- Average transaction value
- Daily/weekly customer count
- Peak hours and days
- Staff count and turnover
- Actual revenue figures
- Customer retention/repeat rate
