# Sector Lens: Restaurant & Food Service

Additional data points to extract when analyzing restaurant and food service businesses — dine-in restaurants, cloud kitchens, cafes, bakeries, QSRs, bars, catering services.

Load this lens during Step 2 when the business type is restaurant/cafe/food service/catering.

---

## Extra Extraction Points (Beyond Universal Framework)

### Menu & Cuisine
- Cuisine type and positioning (fine dining, casual, fast casual, QSR, cloud kitchen)
- Full menu with pricing (categorized: starters, mains, desserts, drinks)
- Signature dishes or chef's specials
- Dietary accommodations (vegan, gluten-free, halal, Jain, keto, allergen info)
- Menu rotation frequency (seasonal, weekly specials, fixed)
- Average price range per person

### Ordering & Delivery
- Online ordering system (own website, third-party, both)
- Delivery platforms present (Zomato, Swiggy, UberEats, DoorDash, own fleet)
- Delivery radius and estimated times
- Table reservation system (Dineout, EazyDiner, OpenTable, phone-only)
- Takeaway/packaging approach
- Pre-order or catering options

### Location & Operations
- Number of outlets and locations
- Seating capacity per location
- Operating hours (lunch, dinner, all-day, late-night)
- Peak hours and days (if mentioned)
- Parking and accessibility
- Ambience positioning (family, romantic, business lunch, party)

### Google Business Profile (Critical for Restaurants)
- GBP rating and review count
- Photo count and quality (food photos, interior, exterior)
- Menu uploaded to GBP
- Response rate to reviews (especially negative)
- Popular times data
- Category accuracy (restaurant type, cuisine tags)

### Trust & Discovery Signals
- Review themes across platforms (food quality, service, ambience, value)
- Food blogger/influencer mentions or collaborations
- Awards, certifications (FSSAI, hygiene ratings, Michelin, Zomato Gold)
- Chef profile or culinary background
- Media coverage or food guide listings

### Competitive Landscape
- Direct competitors in same cuisine and radius
- Price positioning vs local alternatives
- Aggregator ranking and visibility (Zomato/Swiggy listing position)
- Differentiation: cuisine uniqueness, experience, price, location convenience

---

## Common BLANK Fields for Restaurants

These frequently need client intake or direct observation:
- Average order value (dine-in vs delivery)
- Daily covers / order count
- Food cost percentage
- Peak vs off-peak revenue split
- Customer repeat rate
- Delivery vs dine-in revenue ratio
- Staff count and kitchen capacity constraints
