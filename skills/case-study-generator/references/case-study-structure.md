# Case Study Structure — Setup → Problem → Diagnosis → Fix → Result → Lesson

The 6-stage narrative arc shared by all 4 deliverables. Same arc, different platform formats.

## Stage 1 — Setup (the context)

**Goal:** Establish who the client is, what time period, what was at stake. Build credibility through specificity.

**Required elements:**
- Client identifier (named OR anonymized per naming policy)
- Time period (e.g., "90 days", "Q4 2024")
- Baseline numbers (where the client started)
- Industry/niche context (briefly, why this matters)

**Examples:**

✓ Good: "A wellness retreat client in NSW Australia. 90 days. Starting at 21% Meta CTR but 1.2% landing-page conversion. Spending $50/day."

✗ Bad: "A client wanted more leads." (Generic, no specificity, no credibility.)

**Length per platform:**
- LI carousel: 1 slide
- X thread: 2 tweets
- Blog: 1 paragraph (200-300 words)
- IG carousel: 1 slide

## Stage 2 — Problem (what wasn't working)

**Goal:** Name the specific failure. The "why" behind the engagement. Should be a problem the audience also has.

**Required elements:**
- 1-2 specific friction points
- Why standard advice doesn't fix it
- A number that quantifies the problem (e.g., "1.2% conversion" or "47% bounce")

**Examples:**

✓ Good: "Meta was doing its job. The page was not. 21% CTR meant ads were lighting fire — but 1.2% LP conversion meant 99% of clicks left without acting."

✗ Bad: "Conversions were low and we needed to fix it." (No specificity, no quantification.)

**Length per platform:**
- LI carousel: 1 slide
- X thread: 1-2 tweets
- Blog: 1 H2 section, 300-400 words
- IG carousel: 1-2 slides

## Stage 3 — Diagnosis (where the friction was)

**Goal:** Show the analytical work. Why the problem existed. Pattern recognition the audience can copy.

**Required elements:**
- The investigative process (what was looked at)
- The 1-2 root causes identified
- Bonus: a contrarian insight ("most people would optimize X, but the actual cause was Y")

**Examples:**

✓ Good: "Three things were broken. Hero was 15 words of buzzwords. Phone number buried in footer. Form had 6 fields when 2 would do. Wellness clients wanted to talk before they booked — the page wasn't letting them."

**Length per platform:**
- LI carousel: 1-2 slides
- X thread: 1-2 tweets
- Blog: 1 H2 section, 300-400 words
- IG carousel: 1 slide

## Stage 4 — Fix (the 3 specific changes)

**Goal:** Concrete actions. THE actionable section. Should make the audience say "I can do that."

**Required elements:**
- Exactly 3 changes (not more — audience glaze at 4+)
- Each change is specific + measurable
- Time investment per change (e.g., "Cut from 15 words to 7. 5 min change.")

**Format:** numbered list, one change per line/bullet/slide

**Example (Thrive Retreats):**
1. Hero headline cut from 15 words to 7. A hero is real estate, not a paragraph.
2. Phone number moved above the fold. Wellness clients often want to talk before they book.
3. Form trimmed from 6 fields to 2. Name and phone. The rest came by email after.

**Length per platform:**
- LI carousel: 3 slides (one per change) OR 1 slide with 3 bullets
- X thread: 3 tweets (one per change)
- Blog: 1 H2 section with 3 H3 subsections, 600-800 words total
- IG carousel: 3 slides (one per change, visual-heavy)

## Stage 5 — Result (the numbers)

**Goal:** Pay off the hook. Show the outcome with the headline metric.

**Required elements:**
- The headline metric (must match the hook from Slide 1 / Tweet 1 / H1 / Slide 1)
- 1-2 supporting metrics
- Time period (if not already in Setup)

**Examples:**

✓ Good: "188% more Meta sales. 37% lower cost per sale. 90 days. Same audience, same creative, same budget."

✗ Bad: "We saw great results and the client was happy." (No numbers, no specificity.)

**Length per platform:**
- LI carousel: 1 slide (BIG numbers)
- X thread: 1-2 tweets
- Blog: 1 H2 section, 200-300 words (with description of trend chart)
- IG carousel: 1 slide (BIGGEST numbers)

## Stage 6 — Lesson (what this means for you)

**Goal:** Generalize the insight. Make it useful to readers who aren't this specific client.

**Required elements:**
- A re-stated principle (1-2 lines)
- Why it applies to the audience's likely situation
- Avoid "you should..." prescriptive tone — use "this means..." reflective tone

**Examples:**

✓ Good: "Most 'ad problems' are landing page problems wearing ad-problem clothes. Before you optimize creative, ask whether the page is closing what the ad opens."

✗ Bad: "You should always optimize your landing pages first." (Prescriptive, generic.)

**Length per platform:**
- LI carousel: 1 slide
- X thread: 1 tweet
- Blog: 1 H2 section, 200-300 words
- IG carousel: 1 slide

## Optional Stage 7 — Soft CTA

**Goal:** Invite engagement without engagement bait. Open a question, not a request for likes.

**Required elements:**
- 1 sentence
- A question or observation, NOT "drop a comment" / "tag someone"

**Examples:**

✓ Good: "What's the one LP change you keep avoiding?"

✗ Bad: "Comment YES if this resonates with you!" (Engagement bait — banned by voice guide.)

**Length per platform:**
- LI carousel: 1 slide (subtle)
- X thread: optional last tweet
- Blog: 1 closing line
- IG carousel: 1 slide
