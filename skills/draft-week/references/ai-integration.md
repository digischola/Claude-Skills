# AI-Theme Integration (How to Talk About Using AI Without Sounding Cringey)

Source: Perplexity research 2026-04-18.

AI is a **theme across all three pillars**, never the pillar itself. The brand positioning (LOCKED): "Ex-Google performance marketer, AI-native solo operator." Key word = *operator*, not *evangelist*.

## Patterns that work

1. **Quantified time saved.** Specific hours and minutes, not "faster than ever."
2. **Named tools and workflows.** "Perplexity → Claude → Remotion" beats "I use AI tools."
3. **Before / after outputs shown.** Raw input alongside the final human-approved output.
4. **Admitting limitations openly.** "The AI produced X. I rewrote Y because it missed Z." Builds credibility.
5. **AI as employee, not magician.** "I handed the data processing over to Claude" frames AI as a capable colleague, not a miracle.

## Patterns that fail

1. **The prompt flex.** Sharing a 500-word mega-prompt with no business outcome attached.
2. **Vague evangelism.** "AI is changing everything for marketing." Dead on arrival in 2026.
3. **Endless tool-stack posts.** Listing 20 tools with no use case for any single one.
4. **"One weird AI trick" clickbait.** "This AI prompt changed my freelance business." Suppressed.
5. **Unqualified claims.** "AI wrote my entire landing page." Nobody believes it and the audience punishes the implausibility.

## Sentence-level swaps

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "AI completely transformed my workflow." | "I handed the raw data processing over to Claude." |
| "Here is the ultimate prompt to write copy." | "I used this specific constraint in Perplexity to filter the data." |
| "Artificial intelligence created this landing page." | "I wireframed the structure. Lovable handled the frontend code." |
| "AI will replace marketers." | "AI replaced the parts of my job I hated. It did not replace the strategy." |
| "Using AI to automate everything." | "Using Claude Code to automate the 3 parts of my workflow that burned 80% of my time." |

## Integration rules for this brand

1. **Always name the tool.** Claude Code, Perplexity, Lovable, Remotion, Wabo, Canva. Generic "AI" is lazy.
2. **Always quantify.** Time saved (minutes/hours), money saved ($), or quality gained (specific outcome).
3. **Always keep the human in the edit seat.** "I still write the final copy" / "I approve every draft" / "Claude wrote the outline, I rewrote the hook." The human-in-loop signal matters.
4. **Max 1 AI-themed post per 3 posts.** Otherwise the brand becomes "AI guy" and loses the operator/credibility edge.
5. **Pair AI with credential.** The combo "I managed $1B at Google, now I use Claude Code for small-budget SMB audits" lands harder than either alone.

## Example posts (reference templates)

### Example 1: Research-to-visual workflow (LinkedIn text)
> I used Perplexity to pull the latest wellness-retreat search trends for a client.
>
> Instead of spending three hours reading reports, I fed the summary into Claude to generate a dashboard wireframe.
>
> Total time: 14 minutes.
>
> The client got a customized strategy doc before their kids finished breakfast. I kept the afternoon for the actual campaign work.
>
> Tools that replaced 3 hours for me this week: [list]. What replaced hours for you?

**Why it works:** names tools, quantifies time, shows concrete output (strategy doc), ends with a question that invites other operators.

### Example 2: Solo-agency multiplier (LinkedIn carousel or long post)
> Most solo operators stay small because they execute manually.
>
> Last week I treated my marketing funnel like a codebase.
>
> Claude Code automated the deep research for a new B2B client without me leaving the terminal. I still wrote the final positioning. But the AI handled the data extraction.
>
> Result: 4 hours of client research in 35 minutes. Same depth. Fewer errors.
>
> What is the one repetitive task in your week you could hand over this month?

**Why it works:** specific outcome (4 hrs → 35 min), preserves human edit seat, ends with applicable question.

### Example 3: Small-budget optimization (X thread, 5 tweets)
> 1/ When you have $30/day for Meta ads, you cannot afford week-long A/B testing.
>
> 2/ I run client past-ad data through Perplexity first. It identifies the angles competitors are missing before I spend a dollar.
>
> 3/ Exact prompt structure: "Compare brand X ad creative last 90 days. Flag 3 gaps vs industry norm."
>
> 4/ That 10-minute check informs the first 2 ad variants. Hit rate on those is roughly 3x my cold-start average.
>
> 5/ This is how I used to open a media plan at Google. The logic is the same. The budget is 1000x smaller.

**Why it works:** credential (Google) paired with scale-down use case (SMB), names tools, shows a concrete prompt structure, ends with a credibility anchor.

## What to NEVER post

- "Here are 7 ChatGPT prompts every marketer needs."
- "AI is the future. Adapt or die."
- "I built this entire business with AI." (without showing the business output)
- "Anthropic just released X feature." (unless you have a specific use case to demonstrate)
- Generic AI tool roundups.
