## Mimicry rules (critical)

- **Inspiration, never copy.** Mimic format structure, not actual content. Peep Laja's contrast pattern is a format; "amateurs focus on X" is his specific claim. Use the format, make your own claim.
- **Credit where natural.** When a format is iconic (Dickie's 3x3, Jack Butcher's minimalist visuals), a sincere "inspired by @X" is OK in the first post using that format. Drop the credit after the format becomes yours.
- **Test format fit, don't blindly replicate.** Julian Shapiro's 20-tweet threads work for his 200K audience. For a 1,600-connection LinkedIn account, shorter threads will perform better. Start with the structure, scale the ambition.
- **Never mimic their personality.** Mimic their format, keep your voice. Peep Laja is harsh; Mayank's voice is direct-and-honest. Harry Dry is playful; Mayank's is analytical-dry. Don't force someone else's personality.
