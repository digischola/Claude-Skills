## 1. Headline Rules

### Universal Rules:
- **6-12 words, 60-100 characters** — core benefit early, qualifier at end
- **Clarity over cleverness** — benefit-driven, positive framing outperforms by 27-40%
- **Headline + subhead pair always** — headline hooks, subhead adds specificity
- **Mobile: single short value-prop line** — multi-idea headlines fail on small screens
- **People absorb first and last 3 words** — put benefit density there

### Headline Formulas by Page Type:

#### Retreat/Wellness Booking ($300-2000)

| # | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Outcome + Timeframe + Container | "Reset your mind and body in 5 days at our coastal yoga retreat" |
| 2 | Escape Pain + Destination Relief | "Leave burnout behind and feel energized again in Kingscliff" |
| 3 | Identity + Specific Benefit | "For busy professionals who want to recharge without leaving Australia" |
| 4 | Experiential Promise + Safety | "Experience deep nervous-system reset with expert-led daily yoga and breathwork" |
| 5 | Limited-Spots Intimacy + Transformation | "Only 12 guests: Reclaim your calm in an intimate coastal retreat" |
| 6 | Seasonal/Cyclical Framing | "Spring retreat to reset your body and soften into stillness" |

#### Workshop/Event ($50-300)

| # | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Topic + Outcome + Format | "Transform your backbends in a 3-hour alignment workshop" |
| 2 | Problem -> Solution | "Struggling with anxiety? Learn calming breathwork in this live workshop" |
| 3 | Date-Anchored Outcome | "By Sunday, you'll know a 20-minute home practice you can actually stick to" |
| 4 | One Big Promise | "The Burnout-to-Balance Half-Day Reset" |
| 5 | Audience + Objection Handling | "Beginners who think they're 'not flexible enough' will discover yoga that meets you where you are" |

#### Lead Generation (Free Consult/Enquiry)

| # | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Free Clarity + Time Framing | "Get a 20-minute clarity call to plan your ideal retreat" |
| 2 | Assessment + Outcome | "Find out if this retreat is right for your body before you book" |
| 3 | Pathway Language | "Your path to sustainable energy starts with a free wellness consultation" |
| 4 | Social Proof + Access | "Join 250+ women who started with a free stress-reset consultation" |

#### Teacher Training/Certification ($2000+)

| # | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Identity Shift + Credential | "Become a certified yoga teacher with our 200-hour immersive training" |
| 2 | Career Outcome + Accreditation | "Start teaching vinyasa confidently with Yoga Alliance-registered training" |
| 3 | Time-Bound Career Milestone | "In 6 months, qualify to teach group classes and retreats worldwide" |
| 4 | Current Identity -> Next Level | "For dedicated practitioners ready to step into teaching" |
| 5 | Inner + Outer Outcome | "Deepen your personal practice and gain a professional 200-hour certification" |

### Subheadline Rules:

**Role:** Add specificity, proof, and risk-reduction under a concise headline.
**Length:** 15-25 words. Headline + subhead readable in under 5-6 seconds.

**Patterns:**
1. **Outcome + Mechanism** — "Through daily yoga, guided meditations, and nourishing meals, you will reset your nervous system."
2. **Date + Location + Format** — "5 days in Kingscliff, NSW, with ocean-view rooms and twice-daily practice."
3. **Social Proof** — "Join 300+ practitioners who've deepened their practice."
4. **Urgency/Availability** — "Next cohort starts June 15. Limited to 12 participants."
5. **Risk Reducer** — "Apply with no payment due until acceptance."

## 2. Section Copy Frameworks

### Copy Density Rules (all sections):
- **40-80 words per block** before visual break on mobile (up to 120 on desktop)
- **1-3 sentence paragraphs** — break any paragraph exceeding 3 lines on phone
- **10-20 words per sentence** average
- **Visual break every 2-4 paragraphs** (subhead, icon, image, divider)

### Hero Section
**Job:** Answer "What is this?", "Why should I care?", "What do I do next?" in 3-6 seconds.
**Word count:** 40-70 words (excluding nav and button)

**Structure:**
1. Headline: 6-12 words, transformation-oriented
2. Subheadline: 15-30 words — mechanism, specificity, proof/urgency
3. Supporting line: 1-2 bullets or single sentence with social proof
4. Primary CTA + microcopy

### Transformation/Story Section
**Job:** Make visitors see themselves in "before" and desire the "after."
**Word count:** 120-240 words

**Structure:**
1. Empathy paragraph — describe the "before" state (2-3 sentences)
2. Bridge sentence — introduce the offering as turning point
3. "After" paragraph — paint the transformed state (2-3 sentences or bullets)
4. Optional embedded testimonial

**Tone:** Gentle empathy, not shame. "If stress and screens are wearing you down" not "You're destroying your health."

### Benefits Section
**Job:** Translate features into concrete life changes.
**Word count:** 80-180 words

**Structure:**
1. Intro sentence anchoring to outcomes
2. 3-6 benefit bullets, each with short heading + one clarifying phrase
3. Optional closing sentence linking back to visitor's situation

**Rule:** Every benefit follows "You will [feel/do/be]" framing, not "We offer [feature]."

### What's Included Section
**Job:** Justify price by itemizing inclusions as value.
**Word count:** 100-200 words

**Structure:**
1. Framing sentence ("Everything is taken care of")
2. 5-10 inclusions grouped logically (accommodation, food, teaching, extras)
3. Optional "not included" list for expectations

**Value-stacking:** Mention market value sparingly ("Worth over $X if booked separately"). Don't over-engineer — feels too "internet-marketery" for wellness audiences.

### Who This Is For Section
**Job:** Help visitors self-qualify. Address "Am I [good/fit/spiritual] enough?"
**Word count:** 80-160 words

**Structure:**
1. Intro sentence
2. "This is for you if..." (3-5 bullets)
3. "It's not for you if..." (2-3 bullets)

### Pricing Section
**Job:** Present price as investment, not cost. Reduce anxiety.
**Word count:** 60-140 words

**Structure:**
1. Framing sentence tying price to transformation
2. Clear price/range + payment options
3. 3-5 bullets reminding of key inclusions
4. Optional guarantee or refund policy

**Language rules:**
- Use "investment" for retreats/trainings (frames spend as gain, not loss)
- Use "tuition" for certifications/training programs
- Payment plan: show monthly amount AND total transparently
- Early bird: anchor to real deadline ("Save $200 when you book before May 15")

### FAQ Section
**Job:** Handle objections in low-pressure format.
**Word count:** 200-400 words

**Structure:**
- 6-10 questions, 2-4 sentence answers each
- Order by objection weight: safety → difficulty → logistics → money → social anxiety

**Key questions by type:**
- Retreat: "I'm a beginner — will I keep up?", "What if I have dietary needs?", "Can I come alone?", "Cancellation policy?", "How do I get there?"
- Workshop: "What should I bring?", "Is this for beginners?", "What if I can't make it?"
- Training: "What are the entry requirements?", "How much study time?", "Is it accredited?", "Payment plans?"

### Final CTA Section
**Job:** Close the argument. Summarize + urgency + single action.
**Word count:** 40-90 words

**Structure:**
1. 1-2 sentence recap of core benefit
2. 1 sentence on timing/availability
3. CTA button + supportive microcopy

## 3. CTA Copy Rules

### First-Person Default:
First-person CTAs outperform by up to 90% in click-through.
- "Reserve my spot" > "Reserve your spot"
- "Start my journey" > "Start your journey"
- "Schedule my free consultation" > "Schedule a consultation"

### Action + Benefit Pattern:
- "See retreat dates and pricing" (clarity, low commitment)
- "Get my retreat info pack" (lead magnet)
- "Apply for teacher training" (high-commitment)
- "Book my discovery call" (lead gen)

### Length: 2-5 words. Must fit single line on mobile.

### CTA Evolution Down the Page:
| Position | Commitment Level | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | Low — explore | "See retreat details" / "Download guide" |
| After benefits/included | Medium — evaluate | "Check dates and availability" |
| Pricing & final | High — commit | "Reserve my spot" / "Apply for this cohort" |

**Rule:** One primary conversion goal per page. Different phrasings OK, but never competing actions.

### CTA Supporting Microcopy:

**Risk reducers (highest impact):**
- "Fully refundable up to 30 days before start"
- "Cancel anytime before [date]"
- "No credit card required" (+19% sign-ups in one test)

**Time reducers:**
- "Takes under 2 minutes"
- "3-step form"

**Privacy reassurers:**
- "No spam ever. Unsubscribe in one click"
- "We never share your details"

**Social proof:**
- "Join 200+ guests who've attended"
- "Trusted by teachers from 5 countries"

## 4. Emotional Arc

The page follows an emotional sequence:

| Phase | Sections | Emotion | Technique |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Hook | Hero, Transformation | Aspiration, empathy, relief | Sensory language, "before" recognition |
| 2. Justify | Benefits, What's Included, Program | Logic, value, clarity | Features-as-benefits, structure, specifics |
| 3. Trust | Social Proof, Credentials, FAQ | Safety, confidence, belonging | Testimonials, credentials, objection handling |
| 4. Close | Pricing, Final CTA | Gentle urgency, commitment | Real scarcity, investment framing, risk reversal |

**Rule:** Decisions initiated emotionally, justified logically. Never lead with price or logistics.

## 5. Wellness-Specific Language

### Power Verbs (ranked by wellness resonance):
- **Calm/Safety:** safe, gentle, guided, held, supported, nourishing
- **Ease/Relief:** unwind, soften, release, melt, restore, breathe
- **Transformation:** reclaim, reset, realign, deepen, discover, remember, come home
- **Social proof:** trusted, recommended, loved, chosen, joined

### Verbs to Avoid:
- "Crush", "dominate", "hack", "grind" — wrong energy
- "Fix", "cure", "heal" — medical claims territory
- "Should", "must", "need to" — pressure language

### Sensory Language (use in transformation/story sections):
- **Sight:** mountains, ocean, candlelight, sunrise, greenery
- **Sound:** birds, silence, temple bells, waves, breath
- **Touch:** mat under hands, sun on skin, warm tea in palms
- **Taste:** herbal tea, local produce, nourishing meals
- **Smell:** incense, forest, sea air, essential oils

### Community Language:
- DO: "small supportive group", "like-minded people", "community of practitioners"
- DON'T: "tribe" (cliche, cultural appropriation concerns), "family" (too intimate for strangers)

### Spiritual vs Practical Tone:
- **Mainstream/corporate audiences:** lead with practical benefits (better sleep, less anxiety, clearer focus). Introduce spiritual concepts gently with explanations.
- **Experienced practitioners:** some Sanskrit and spiritual framing OK if explained.
- **Never:** over-promise enlightenment, miraculous cures, or life-changing transformations without evidence.

### Urgency Without Manipulation:
- "We keep groups intimate (max 12 participants) so we can support you fully"
- "Spring cohort applications close April 30"
- "Next training runs again in 2027"
- Waitlist as social proof: "Currently full — join the waitlist"
- **Never:** countdown timers that reset, fake scarcity on unlimited offers

## 6. Readability Rules

### Target: Grade 6-8 Flesch-Kincaid (even for educated audiences)
- Simpler copy reduces cognitive load → higher conversion
- Wellness audiences often arrive mentally fatigued → simplicity even more critical

### Sentence Rules:
- Average 10-18 words per sentence
- Mix lengths: short punchy sentences + slightly longer explanatory ones
- Occasional fragments OK for emphasis ("For you.") — but don't overuse in high-ticket contexts

### Paragraph Rules:
- 1-3 sentences max (especially mobile)
- Break any paragraph exceeding 3 lines on phone screen
- Use bullets for 3+ parallel items

### Power Words with Measured Impact:
| Word | Conversion Lift |
|---|---|
| "You" / first-person | +26% |
| "Free" | +22% |
| "Instantly" / "immediate" | +18% |
| "Guaranteed" | +15-25% |
| "Proven" / "trusted" | significant (unquantified) |

**Rule:** Weave naturally. Stacking power words feels hypey and damages wellness brand trust.

## 7. Microcopy Library

### Form Labels:
- "Full name" (single field, not split first/last)
- "Email address" or "Where should we send your retreat details?"
- "Phone (optional)" — making phone optional nearly doubles conversion in some tests
- Help text: "We'll only use this to share retreat details and travel info"

### Error Messages:
- State what's wrong + how to fix in plain language
- "That email doesn't look right. Please check for extra spaces or typos."
- Never blame the user. Neutral, friendly tone.

### Success/Confirmation:
- "You're in. Check your inbox in the next 2 minutes for your retreat guide."
- "Thank you for your application. We'll review it within 48 hours and email you next steps."
- Always set expectation for what happens next + when.

### Navigation Microcopy:
- Sticky header labels: literal not clever ("Schedule", "Pricing", "FAQ", "Reserve")
- Scroll prompt: "Scroll to see what's included" (with down arrow)
- Back-to-top: "Back to top" or "Back to dates"

### Trust Microcopy:
- Badge captions: "Yoga Alliance Certified", "As featured in [publication]"
- Testimonial attribution: "Maya S., Sydney — 2024 retreat guest" (name, city, context)
- Guarantee: **specific > vague**. "Full refund if cancelled 30 days before" > "100% satisfaction guaranteed"
- Payment security: "Secure payment via Stripe" + card logos near payment button
