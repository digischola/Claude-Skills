# Research Dimensions (11 Sections)

Every market research output covers these 11 dimensions. The Perplexity prompt requests all of them; the analysis tags every data point per the accuracy protocol; the report and dashboard organize findings by dimension.

---

## 1. Market Size & Demand

**What to uncover:**
- TAM, SAM, SOM for the industry in the target country/region
- Market size, revenue, CAGR, projections (current year through +5 years)
- Transaction/activity volume per year (local, national, global context)
- % of transactions involving professional services vs DIY/in-house
- Growth drivers (3-5 industry-specific)
- Headwinds (3-5 industry-specific)
- Commercial real estate correlation (for B2B/local services)
- Data from authoritative sources (Stats agencies, CBRE, JLL, IBISWorld, etc.)

**Why it matters for marketing:** Sets realistic budget expectations, identifies market timing, and determines if the market supports paid advertising at meaningful scale.

## 2. Porter's Five Forces

**What to uncover:**
- Competitive rivalry — fragmented or consolidated? How many direct competitors?
- Threat of new entrants — barriers (capital, certifications, trust, compliance)
- Threat of substitutes — DIY alternatives, technology substitutes, adjacent services
- Buyer power — procurement processes, price sensitivity, vendor switching
- Supplier power — labor dependence, equipment, scaling constraints

**Why it matters for marketing:** Reveals where the business has leverage and where it's vulnerable. Shapes positioning strategy and competitive messaging.

## 3. PESTEL Analysis (Country-Specific)

**What to uncover:**
- Political/Legal: regulations, government procurement, compliance requirements
- Economic: interest rates, commercial activity, budget cycles, recession impact
- Social: workforce/consumer behavior shifts (hybrid work, demographics, lifestyle trends)
- Technological: technology adoption that increases or decreases service demand
- Environmental: sustainability regulations, ESG requirements, waste management
- Legal/Compliance: insurance, liability, industry-specific legal obligations

**Why it matters for marketing:** Identifies external forces that create urgency (compliance deadlines, regulatory changes) or opportunity (technology shifts, demographic trends) for ad messaging.

## 4. SWOT Analysis

**What to uncover:**
- Strengths: specialist vs generalist positioning, unique capabilities, track record
- Weaknesses: market awareness, sales cycle challenges, revenue model constraints
- Opportunities: underserved verticals, geographic expansion, adjacent services
- Threats: technology disruption, DIY competition, economic conditions

**Why it matters for marketing:** Directly informs ad angles (lead with strengths), objection handling (address weaknesses), and targeting priority (chase opportunities).

## 5. Competitive Landscape

**What to uncover:**
- Top 10-15 competitors: name, location, positioning, differentiators, approximate size
- Positioning strategies: price, enterprise, full-service, niche, bundled
- Active ad presence: who's running Google/Meta ads, what keywords, what offers
- Lead magnets and CTAs used across the industry
- Competitive saturation on target platform — crowded or underserved?
- SEO presence: who ranks organically for key terms
- The "invisible competitor": DIY/in-house alternative and its failure rate

**Why it matters for marketing:** Determines differentiation angle, identifies messaging gaps to exploit, and reveals budget-level competition.

## 6. Search Demand & Keyword Landscape

**What to uncover:**
- All relevant keyword clusters with search volume estimates
- CPC estimates by keyword group in target market
- Long-tail opportunities with commercial intent
- Seasonal search trends (fiscal cycles, industry cycles, weather)
- Adjacent/intent-signal keywords
- Negative keywords to exclude

**Why it matters for marketing:** Directly shapes Google Ads keyword strategy, ad group structure, and budget allocation. For Meta, informs audience interest targeting.

## 7. Platform Ad Benchmarks & Unit Economics

**What to uncover:**

**Google Ads (when applicable):**
- CPC benchmarks for industry keywords in target market
- CTR, conversion rate, CPL, CPA benchmarks
- Estimated monthly budget for meaningful impression share
- Phone call vs form fill conversion comparison
- Google Business Profile / Maps role in discovery

**Meta Ads (when applicable):**
- CPM, CPC, CTR benchmarks for the industry
- Conversion rate, CPA, ROAS benchmarks
- Best performing ad formats and creative types
- Creative fatigue timeline
- Audience size estimates for key segments

**Unit Economics (both):**
- Average deal size by customer segment
- Customer lifetime value, repeat rate, referral rate
- Full funnel: cost per click → lead → qualified opportunity → closed deal → ROI

**Why it matters for marketing:** Sets KPI targets, budget recommendations, and ROI projections. Without benchmarks, there's no way to know if campaign performance is good or bad.

## 8. Buyer Personas & Purchase Journey

**What to uncover:**
- Decision makers: titles, roles, responsibilities
- Decision-making unit: who researches, shortlists, signs off
- Top industries/segments that need this service most
- Company size segments and procurement processes
- Key purchase triggers (events, timelines, pain points)
- Top buyer concerns and objections
- Vendor evaluation process and criteria
- Average sales cycle length

**Why it matters for marketing:** Shapes ad copy (speak to the decision maker's language), targeting (reach the right job titles/interests), and landing page design (address the right objections).

## 9. Channel Partner & Referral Ecosystem

**What to uncover:**
- Who refers business in this industry (partners, adjacent service providers)
- Industry associations and certifications that drive trust
- Procurement platforms or vendor marketplaces used
- Partnership opportunities for lead generation

**Why it matters for marketing:** Identifies non-paid acquisition channels and partnership-driven growth opportunities. Can inform Display/partner targeting on Google Ads.

## 10. Underserved Markets & Blue Ocean Opportunities

**What to uncover:**
- Geographic gaps with high demand but few specialists
- Vertical gaps: segments needing the service but underserved
- Service gaps: adjacent services that could be bundled
- Digital marketing gaps: competitors weak on specific platforms or channels
- Adjacent service opportunities for expansion

**Why it matters for marketing:** Reveals the easiest wins — where competition is lowest and demand is real. Shapes which verticals and geographies to target first.

## SCOPE BOUNDARY — Strategic Recommendations are NOT a market-research dimension

The skill historically carried an 11th "Strategic Recommendations for Ad Platform(s)" dimension covering campaign structure, budget allocation, ad copy angles, conversion actions, phased plan. **That belongs to `paid-media-strategy`, the next skill in the pipeline — not here.** This boundary was reasserted 2026-04-30 after a scope-creep audit on the ISKM Weekend Love Feast run.

market-research stops at Dimension 10. The deliverable's role is to produce the **research substrate** (market size, forces, PESTEL, SWOT, competitors, keywords, benchmarks, personas, channels, blue ocean) that paid-media-strategy then converts into a campaign plan. The dashboard's "what's next" section should be a forward-pointer to paid-media-strategy, NOT a campaign timeline with budget splits.

If the analyst feels strong about a strategic implication, the right move is a **one-line "Marketing Implication"** at the end of each of dimensions 1-10 (calling out the takeaway), NOT a separate Dimension 11 prescribing the campaign. Cross-skill connections live in the wiki + the strategy.md handoff line, not in market-research's report.

---

## Cross-Referencing Across Dimensions

Findings in one dimension should inform others, AND inform what paid-media-strategy will recommend downstream:
- Market size (1) validates whether the brief's budget is realistic
- Porter's (2) reveals if buyer power is high — shapes pricing/positioning posture
- PESTEL (3) compliance requirements become ad-messaging guard-rails
- SWOT (4) strengths become ad-copy angle candidates (paid-media-strategy chooses)
- Competitors (5) gaps inform positioning leverage points
- Keywords (6) volume + benchmarks (7) costs feed paid-media-strategy budget math
- Buyer personas (8) concerns shape landing-page + creative strategy (paid-media-strategy + landing-page-builder)
- Referral ecosystem (9) informs partnership / non-paid channel mix
- Blue ocean (10) prioritises which verticals/geos paid-media-strategy targets first

Every finding should trace to a **practical marketing implication** — but the implication is a one-line takeaway, NOT a campaign prescription.
